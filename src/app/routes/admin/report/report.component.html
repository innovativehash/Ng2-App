Methdology

Leveraging 500+ IT Due Diligence projects, DealValue evaluates Information Technology risks and opportunities across multiple dimensions, including the following:

IT Strategy

  • Alignment of the IT strategy with the businessstrategy

Application Portfolio

  • Stability and scalability of the application portfolio
  • Use of commercial packages vs. Custom applications
  • Integrated vs. standalone applications
  • Level of process automation

IT Projects

  • Capital, timing and resources requirements tocomplete the current and future projects
  • Potential risks of failure (delays, additional costs)

IT Spending

  • Reasonableness of IT spending levels compared tobenchmarks
  • Run-rate (Opex) vs. One-time Costs (Capex)

IT Organization

  • Leadership and appropriate skillsets
  • In house resources vs. effective use of third partysupport

IT Infrastructure

  • Scalability and redundancy of the IT infrastructure
  • Preparedness for disaster recovery, changemanagement, business continuity, and servicemanagement

Approach for Increasing the IT Value & Decreasing IT Risk

IT Strategy

  • Develop stakeholder analysis
  • Review/set the vision, mission, and goals of the broader organization
  • Understand external requirements (legal, regulatory, etc.)
  • Review internal organizational requirements, commitments and SLA’s
  • Review overarching Information Technology Strategy
  • Develop organizational overview including any relevant outside parties:
    • Key vendors & service providers
    • Outsourced areas
  • Gather any (internal or external) “customer” satisfaction feedback or, if needed, initiatecustomer survey

Baseline of Current State

  • Issue information request & conduct interviews
    • Documentation on arch, systems, software, processes, run books, etc.
    • Historic data capture
    • Client-specific interview guides; qualitative information
    • Baseline of current spend and cost structure
  • Baseline IT functions:
    • Applications, Infrastructure, Service Management, Data Management, Security & Technology Tools
  • Baseline system lifecycle from planning to deployment
  • Baseline IT Governance, Processes, and PMO, e.g. strategy, governance, roles & responsibilities, policies, procedures, architecture
  • Baseline personnel management structure and approaches including change management techniques

Analysis and Evalutaion

  • Compare baseline information to internal priorities and commitments and identify gaps,with a focus on:
    • Spend
    • Resources
    • “Customer” needs
  • Evaluate baseline findings versus other guidelines, standards, and best practices
    • Adoption of external “best practices” or performance metrics
    • Following of Industry guidelines or standards and any internal guidelines or standards
    • Use of standard tools
  • Review baseline findings with our functional experts and identify improvement opportunities
    • By IT function
    • IT security
    • Comparison with proprietary benchmarks

Final Recommendations

  • Compile results of “customer” survey (if conducted)
    • Link finding to relevant underlying IT operations
  • Confirm and validate assessments with client workshop
    • Confirm findings with client
    • Revise assessment
  • Develop recommendations
    • Incorporation of McGladrey Consulting expertise
    • Revision for client viability
    • Identification of “quick hits”, medium-term opportunities, long-term opportunities
  • Advance recommendations into actionable roadmap with project plans
    • Develop business cases, if needed
  • Present final report
    • Confirm client acceptance
    • Conduct client satisfaction / feedback survey
